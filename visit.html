Top attractions

Scone Palace

Located just 2 miles outside of Perth Scone Palace is a must see for any trip to Perth.
The Palace, once home to Scottish Royalty, is now a top tourist destination. Tours of the Palace and beautiful gardens are available and the Palace regularly hosts events that aren't to be missed.

https://www.scone-palace.co.uk/

Perth Museum

Following major redevelopment Perth Museum opened it's doors in March 2024.
The museum boasts a number of permanent exhibits as well as special events throughout the year highlighting Perth's significance and culture as a key region in Scottish History.
The crown jewel of the museum is the famous "Stone of Destiny" on which many Scottish and British kings have been crowned.

Blackwatch Castle and Museum

The Blackwatch Castle and Museum is a top attraction in Perth. Located in the city, the Museum explores the history of the famous Blackwatch regiment from it's formation in 1739 to it's amalgamation in 2006.
The 5-star attraction brings to life the work of the Blakwatch regiment, and the key role it played in many military conflicts across history.

In the city

Perth Art Gallery

Inside the iconic building the Perth Art Gallery boasts an impressive permenant collection which highlights Perth's history and incredible natural landscapes.

Perth Playhouse

Since it's opening 1933 the independent cinema has been at the heart of the community.
With seven screens showing a variety of films from new blockbusters to old classics the cinema is a hit with tourists and locals alike.

Perth Theatre and Concert Hall

Whether it's a night at the theatre or a breathtaking musical performance the Perth theatre and concert hall is the best of the cultural experiences on offer in Perth. Catching a performance should be on the list for any trip to the city.

North and South Inch Parks

If it's a break from the busy streets you're looking for, the two parks in the city offer scenic walks by the river Tay.

Explore Perthshire

Kinnoull Hill

Just outside the city the Kinnoull Hill woodland park offers scenic woodland walks and breathtaking views.

Gateway to the Highlands

Perth is the perfect starting point from which to explore the Scottish countryside. An hours drive takes you well into the highlands, and there are countless walks and scenic locations at your doorstep.

Golf

If it's a round of golf that you're looking for Perthsire is the perfect place. With a number of beautiful courses there is plenty to choose from.
The best of these courses are the three championship courses at Gleneagles Hotel, which hosted the famous Ryder Cup in 2014.
